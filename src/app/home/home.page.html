An important note:
 When describing using the format "From A to B, the price of Toyota cars ______(verb)". Do not use the present-simple tense form of the verb, ie:

- From Monday to Tuesday, the price of Toyota cars go up.

Instead, you should be using the past-simple tense as we are describing an action that has already taken place. For example:

- From Monday to Tuesday, the price of Toyota cars went up. (go is the present-simple form of went)

How to describe a Line Graph:
 1.) Say what the line graph is about (ex: This line graph is about cars)
2.) Describe the trend of the line graph. The trend is a main idea (ex: Toyota cars are volatile in price over the years)
3.) Talk about the changes occurring between periods of time (ex: From 2011 to 2012 Toyoto cars rose in price from 13,000$ to 14,000$, From 2012 to 2013 etc...)
4.) Finish your explanation by making a conclusion about the graph. (ex: In conclusion, Toyota cars are volatile in price at a maximum of 16,000$ to a minimum of 5,000$)

Example of how to describe a Line Graph: What is the price of Apples over time?

Trend:

 The overall trend of this graph shows that Apples rise at 3 dollars maxiumum and lower 2 dollars minimum in price

Description:

 This Line Graph is about the price of Apples. From monday to tuesday the price of Apples rose from 1 to 2 dollars. From tuesday to wednesday it stayed at 2 dollars. From wednesday to thursday it went up from 2 to 3 dollars. Finally from thursday to friday it fell from 3 to 2 dollars.

Conclusion:

 In conclusion, the price of Apples from monday to friday is stable.
How to describe a Pie Chart:
 1.) Say what the chart is about (ex: This chart is about cars)
2.) Describe the trend of the chart. The trend is a main idea (ex: This chart shows that people like Toyota cars the most)
3.) Talk first about the biggest item on the pie chart (ex: Toyota cars are the biggest at 75%)
4.) Talk in descending order about the other items on the pie chart (ex: Next Honda is at 20%. Finally Hyundai the smallest is at 5%)
5.) Finish your explanation by making a conclusion or short summary about the chart. (ex: In conclusion, Toyota cars are the most popular and Hyundai cars are the least popular, while Ford is more popular than Hyundai and less popular than Toyota)

How to describe a Bar graph:
 There are two types of Bar graphs. The first type of bar graph, Type A contains information or data similar to that of a pie-chart but doesn't use percentage to show the proportion of one item to another. This type of bar graph is commonly used to make comparisons between different items by visually contrasting data values between different items. For this type of bar graph you would describe it like a pie-chart.

The second type of bar graph Type B is similar to that of a line-graph by plotting the variation of data in a set of members of the graph over a specified period of time. This type of graph is commonly used to show the trend of an item or items through the use of bars instead of lines over a duration of time. For this type of bar-graph you would describe it like a line-graph.
