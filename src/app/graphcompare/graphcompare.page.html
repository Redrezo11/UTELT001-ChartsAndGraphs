Graph Comparisons
An important note:
 When making comparisons, you should use comparative and superlative adjectives. (ie: iphone 11 is more expensive than iphone 1. iphone 11 is the most expensive)

Alot of the times it is useful to use the format 'A is more than B', 'A is less than B', when comparing two items on the same graph. On two different graphs using this same format, here is an example of how to do it:

Example: A in Graph 1 is more than Graph 2 OR A in Graph 1 is 1 more than Graph 2.

When describing trend(s) between two graphs, it is important to emphasize both the overall differences between them and similarities.

Finally in drawing a conclusion, besides re-iterating the trend(s) that you included in your description it maybe a good idea to make a comment or inference based on the data between the two graphs. (example: people ride motorbikes in the Phillipines more than in Saudi Arabia because cars are expensive for Filipinos.)
How to:
 1.) Say what the 2 graphs are about (ex: These graphs are about cars sold in Saudi Arabia and the UAE)
2.) Describe the trend(s) between the two graphs. The trend is a main idea (ex: More Sports cars are sold in the UAE, while more luxury cars and Japanese cars are sold in Saudi Arabia )
3.) Describe some details between the two graphs to support the trend(s) that you have outlined. (ex: Lamborgini and Ferrari are sold in the UAE more than in Saudi Arabia, while in Saudi Arabia Benz and Toyota are sold more than in the UAE.)
4.) Finish your explanation by making a conclusion about the graph. (ex: In conclusion, People in the UAE buy more sports cars because they like to watch Racing shows, while people in Saudi Arabia like Japanese and Luxury cars more)
